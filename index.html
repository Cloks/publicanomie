Some Thoughts II

I've been replaying Dark Souls III and I'm confident in saying that although the game is good, the level design is bad. The original Dark Souls is a masterclass in building an interconnected world, taking the player on journeys that unlock shortcuts leading back to familiar areas. I think this is best exemplified by the connection between Blighttown and Valley of the Drakes - after a long soujourn to an especially unforgiving corner of the map, the player is suddenly back in familiar territory. Dark Souls III is largely linear and loses this sense of a single world as the player is simply led down a path from one encounter to the next. This isn't helped by the fact that the first major area of the game - the High Wall of Lothric - is completely disconnected from the previous and upcoming areas, requiring a clumsy transition between them. Where Dark Souls III really shines is in the boss fights, giving each boss a second phase that forces the player to learn two different movesets to triumph.

I've also been continually playing Balatro, which is as addictive as ever. I'm trying to get a gold seal on the anaglyph deck which reminds me of a piece I read on Substack about how playing video games immerses you in a conversation that's unintelligible and reductive. I've been reading a lot of random Substack pieces lately because of the infinite scroll aspect, something I miss from social media and forums. I've been off of both for about two years now and while it took some time to adjust to, I feel like it's better for me to not be exposed to the constant swirl of misinformation and deranged opinions. I don't know how much time I wasted fighting windmills online but it's definitely not a negligible amount.
