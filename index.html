Some Thoughts I

I haven't been able to read lately. I keep picking up books, making some marginal progress and putting them down again. I've picked up Vineland by Thomas Pynchon again, and judging by where I stuck the bookmark I made it about a third of the way through last time. It's funny and more immediately readable than his famous doorstoppers, requiring less of a grounding in esoterica to get started. Based on what I've read of Pynchon and what I understand of what I haven't read, his writing can be broadly categorized into big ol' all encompassing doorstoppers and detective novels, of which this falls into the latter category. People tend to recommend The Crying of Lot 49 as a good starting point because it's short but I'm of the opinion that it's easier to start with Inherent Vice, which demands less of the reader. Hell, maybe it's reasonable to make demands of the reader, keep the riff-raff out. Knowing Pynchon though, he wants the riff-raff in, namely Californian bums. I'm also picking at Chris Hedge's American Fascists because I like getting less sleep. Look up dominionists and the seven mountain movement if you want to see where our beautiful US of A is being steered.

The enby has been interested in oaters lately, so I've seen a few for the first time. Pat Garret and Billy the Kid is a new favorite, mostly for the score. I watched it in the redone 50th annivesary cut, which Criterion put together for the recent 4K release. I can't get Knockin' on Heaven's Door out of my head. It was the bi-annual Criterion sale lately and I left the website with a much lighter wallet and a stack of important classic and contemporary films, including a posse of oaters, some I expect to go out of print and some of personal interest. I'm looking forward to rewatching Todd Solondz's Happiness in an anamorphic format and maybe rewatching Antichrist without my fingers over my eyes. Speaking of media, HBOCINEMAXQUIBI is trying to sell off the Looney Tunes IP, which feels like some kind of heresy. I've been submitting bids on a character by character basis and I'm this close to getting use of Henry the Chicken Hawk and Slowpoke Rodriguez, not to mention Nasty Canasta.

I can't stop thinking about how poorly the Harris campaign was run, mostly because we live in the world resulting from that and I keep seeing my "allies" blaming her loss on those dang ol' transexuals wanting to be creepin' and peepin' in public restrooms. I think it may have been her lack of a vision that did her in, as well as not having some sort of clearly communicated benefit from voting for her. I have a special hatred for Third Way and it's funny that they're insisting the candidate who campaigned with Liz Cheney wasn't centrist enough. Bernie's still out there, god bless him, taking AOC along for the ride as far as I know. I see a lot of consternation about how we won't have a republic by the next time we vote but c'mon, with the complete corporate capture of both parties I'm willing to bet (but not too much, again, I spent it all on the Criterion sale) we'll be able to vote for the Tim Ryans and other Ohio cretins in 2020-whatever. Hell, I don't know. Some of this Trump stuff is getting pretty scary but I question the wisdom of anyone who is willing to publicly champion taking healthcare from people with mental illnesses. Some dogs bite, after all.

That feels like enough of a ramble. I'm sure that some sort of form will emerge as I keep pounding away at the keys, as long as I stick with it. Once it has, I'll blow it up and we'll start again. Feels nice to have it just be me and raw code in a competition rather than relying on the dictates of some third party.
